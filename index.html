Mass Shootings in the US, 2017-2018

Caring for our kids is our first job. And until we can honestly say that we're doing enough to keep them safe from harm, including long overdue, common-sense gun safety laws that most Americans want, then we have to change.

— Barack Obama, February 14th, 2018

This visualization tracks mass shootings in the United States from January 1st, 2017 to February 14th, 2018.
Red dots signify a shooting in which more people were injured than killed.
Black dots signify a shooting in which more people were killed than injured.

The control panel in the top right corner of the map allows you to filter shootings by month. Click on each marker to learn more information about each shooting.

Information sourced from Gun Violence Archive.
